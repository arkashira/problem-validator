# Product Requirements Document (PRD) for Problem Validator

## Overview

Problem Validator is a tool designed to help entrepreneurs validate their ideas by providing a systematic approach to identify real problems and measure willingness-to-pay. This product will integrate with the existing Axentx pipeline to ensure that the problems identified are validated and have a real market demand.

## Problem Statement

Entrepreneurs often struggle to validate their ideas before investing significant time and resources into development. Without proper validation, they risk building products that do not solve real problems or that do not have a willing customer base. Problem Validator aims to address this by providing a structured methodology to identify and validate problems, ensuring that the ideas have a high likelihood of success.

## Target Users

- **Entrepreneurs**: Individuals or teams with innovative ideas looking to validate their concepts.
- **Startup Founders**: Founders who want to ensure their ideas have market potential before committing to development.
- **Product Managers**: Professionals who need to validate product ideas before allocating resources.

## Goals

- Provide a systematic approach to identify and validate problems.
- Measure willingness-to-pay to ensure market demand.
- Integrate seamlessly with the existing Axentx pipeline to ensure quality and relevance.
- Reduce the risk of building products that do not solve real problems.

## Key Features

### 1. Problem Identification

- **User Surveys**: Conduct surveys to gather initial insights into potential problems.
- **Interview Scheduling**: Schedule and conduct interviews with potential users to gather detailed feedback.
- **Competitor Analysis**: Analyze competitors to identify gaps and opportunities.

### 2. Problem Validation

- **Data Collection**: Collect and analyze data from surveys and interviews.
- **User Segmentation**: Segment users based on their needs and preferences.
- **Problem Prioritization**: Prioritize problems based on their potential impact and feasibility.

### 3. Willingness-to-Pay Measurement

- **Price Sensitivity Analysis**: Analyze how different price points affect willingness-to-pay.
- **Market Demand Estimation**: Estimate the market demand for the identified problems.
- **Competitive Pricing**: Compare pricing strategies with competitors to ensure competitiveness.

### 4. Integration with Axentx Pipeline

- **Quality Assurance**: Ensure that the identified problems meet the quality standards of the Axentx pipeline.
- **Validation**: Validate the problems using the existing validation processes.
- **Feedback Loop**: Provide feedback to the Axentx pipeline to improve future validations.

## Success Metrics

- **Problem Validation Rate**: Percentage of identified problems that are successfully validated.
- **Willingness-to-Pay Accuracy**: Accuracy of the willingness-to-pay measurements.
- **User Satisfaction**: Feedback from users regarding the usefulness and ease of use of the tool.
- **Integration Success**: Seamless integration with the existing Axentx pipeline.

## Scope

### In-Scope

- Development of a web-based tool for problem identification and validation.
- Integration with existing Axentx pipeline for quality assurance and validation.
- Measurement of willingness-to-pay for identified problems.
- User surveys and interviews to gather data.
- Competitor analysis to identify gaps and opportunities.

### Out-of-Scope

- Development of the actual product solutions for the identified problems.
- Marketing and sales of the validated products.
- Integration with third-party tools not related to problem validation.

## Risks and Mitigations

- **Data Privacy**: Ensure that user data is collected and stored securely.
  - **Mitigation**: Implement robust data privacy measures and comply with relevant regulations.
- **Integration Complexity**: Complexity in integrating with the existing Axentx pipeline.
  - **Mitigation**: Conduct thorough testing and have a dedicated team for integration.
- **User Adoption**: Low adoption rate due to the complexity of the tool.
  - **Mitigation**: Provide comprehensive training and support to users.

## Timeline

- **Phase 1: Planning and Design (2 weeks)**
  - Define requirements and create a detailed design document.
- **Phase 2: Development (8 weeks)**
  - Develop the web-based tool and integrate with the Axentx pipeline.
- **Phase 3: Testing and Validation (4 weeks)**
  - Conduct thorough testing and validation of the tool.
- **Phase 4: Deployment and Feedback (2 weeks)**
  - Deploy the tool and gather feedback from users.
- **Phase 5: Iteration and Improvement (Ongoing)**
  - Continuously improve the tool based on user feedback and market changes.

## Conclusion

Problem Validator aims to provide entrepreneurs with a systematic approach to identify and validate problems, ensuring that their ideas have a high likelihood of success. By integrating with the existing Axentx pipeline, the tool will ensure quality and relevance, reducing the risk of building products that do not solve real problems or do not have a willing customer base.
