# Customer Journey Map
## Aware
### Trigger Event
- **Event**: Entrepreneur stumbles upon a problem while working on a new idea
- **Frequency**: 1-2 times a week
- **Source**: Online research, networking, or personal experience

### Friction Points
- **Lack of systematic approach**: Entrepreneurs often rely on intuition or anecdotal evidence to validate their ideas
- **Insufficient data**: Limited access to market data and customer feedback

### User Emotions
- **Frustration**: Feeling stuck or uncertain about the viability of their idea
- **Curiosity**: Wanting to learn more about the problem and potential solutions

### Opportunities to Delight
- **Relevant resources**: Providing access to relevant market data, customer feedback, and industry reports
- **Personalized guidance**: Offering tailored advice and support to help entrepreneurs validate their ideas

### Metric
- **Awareness**: Number of entrepreneurs who have heard about the product (e.g., social media engagement, website traffic)

## Consider
### Trigger Event
- **Event**: Entrepreneur discovers the product through online research or word-of-mouth
- **Frequency**: 1-2 times a month
- **Source**: Online search, social media, or recommendations from peers

### Friction Points
- **Doubts about effectiveness**: Entrepreneurs may question the product's ability to accurately validate their ideas
- **Concerns about cost**: Worrying about the investment required to use the product

### User Emotions
- **Hope**: Believing that the product could help them validate their idea and reduce uncertainty
- **Apprehension**: Fear of investing time and resources into a product that may not deliver results

### Opportunities to Delight
- **Clear value proposition**: Clearly communicating the product's benefits and how it can help entrepreneurs validate their ideas
- **Social proof**: Sharing success stories and testimonials from existing customers

### Metric
- **Consideration**: Number of entrepreneurs who have added the product to their consideration list (e.g., email sign-ups, free trials)

## Try
### Trigger Event
- **Event**: Entrepreneur decides to try the product after researching and considering it
- **Frequency**: 1-2 times a quarter
- **Source**: Online search, social media, or recommendations from peers

### Friction Points
- **Complexity**: Finding the product's interface and features overwhelming or difficult to use
- **Limited support**: Insufficient documentation, tutorials, or customer support

### User Emotions
- **Excitement**: Eager to try the product and see if it can help them validate their idea
- **Frustration**: Feeling overwhelmed or stuck while using the product

### Opportunities to Delight
- **Intuitive interface**: Designing an easy-to-use interface that minimizes friction and maximizes user engagement
- **Prompt support**: Providing timely and helpful support through various channels (e.g., email, chat, phone)

### Metric
- **Engagement**: Number of entrepreneurs who have actively used the product (e.g., login frequency, feature adoption)

## Adopt
### Trigger Event
- **Event**: Entrepreneur achieves a positive outcome using the product (e.g., validated idea, increased confidence)
- **Frequency**: 1-2 times a year
- **Source**: Product usage, customer support, or word-of-mouth

### Friction Points
- **Cost**: Worrying about the ongoing investment required to use the product
- **Commitment**: Feeling locked into a long-term contract or commitment

### User Emotions
- **Satisfaction**: Feeling confident that the product has helped them validate their idea and achieve their goals
- **Loyalty**: Willing to recommend the product to others and continue using it

### Opportunities to Delight
- **Value-based pricing**: Offering pricing plans that align with the entrepreneur's needs and budget
- **Flexible contracts**: Providing flexible contract options that allow entrepreneurs to adjust their commitment as needed

### Metric
- **Retention**: Number of entrepreneurs who continue to use the product over time (e.g., churn rate, customer lifetime value)

## Expand
### Trigger Event
- **Event**: Entrepreneur achieves significant success using the product (e.g., launched a successful startup, increased revenue)
- **Frequency**: 1-2 times a year
- **Source**: Product usage, customer support, or word-of-mouth

### Friction Points
- **Scalability**: Worrying about the product's ability to scale with their growing business
- **Integration**: Difficulty integrating the product with other tools and systems

### User Emotions
- **Confidence**: Feeling confident that the product can continue to support their growing business
- **Excitement**: Eager to explore new features and capabilities that can help them achieve even greater success

### Opportunities to Delight
- **Scalable infrastructure**: Designing a scalable infrastructure that can support growing businesses
- **Seamless integration**: Providing easy integration options with other tools and systems

### Metric
- **Expansion**: Number of entrepreneurs who expand their use of the product to new areas of their business (e.g., new features, new teams)
